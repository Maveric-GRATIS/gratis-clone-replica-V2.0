DONOR PRIVACY POLICY
G.R.A.T.I.S. (Giving Resources to Achieve Transformative and Impactful Change)
Protecting Your Privacy as a Valued Supporter
Last Updated: January 14, 2026 | Effective: February 14, 2026
1. Our Commitment to Donor Privacy
G.R.A.T.I.S. (Giving Resources to Achieve Transformative and Impactful Change) ("GRATIS") values the trust you place in us when you make a donation. We are committed to respecting your privacy and protecting your personal information. This Donor Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, protect, and share information about our donors.
This policy is designed to comply with:
• General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679
• Dutch data protection laws (Uitvoeringswet AVG)
• ANBI (Algemeen Nut Beogende Instelling) transparency requirements
• US 501(c)(3) donor privacy standards
• Association of Fundraising Professionals (AFP) Donor Bill of Rights
• California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
• Other applicable US state privacy laws
2. Donor Bill of Rights
GRATIS subscribes to the Donor Bill of Rights, which affirms that philanthropy must be based on trust. As a donor, you have the right to:
• Be informed of the organization's mission and how donations are used
• Know the identity of board members and expect prudent governance
• Access the organization's most recent financial statements
• Be assured donations are used for intended purposes
• Receive appropriate acknowledgment and recognition
• Be assured information is handled with respect and confidentiality
• Expect professional relationships with staff
• Be informed if those seeking donations are volunteers or paid professionals
• Know whether solicitors are employed by the organization
• Have the opportunity to remove your name from mailing lists
• Ask questions and receive prompt, truthful answers

4. How We Use Donor Information
4.1 Primary Uses
• Processing your donation and issuing tax receipts
• Acknowledging and thanking you for your gift
• Sending updates about your donation's impact
• Providing information about GRATIS programs and opportunities
• Inviting you to events and engagement opportunities
• Fulfilling legal and regulatory requirements
4.2 Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)
• Contract Performance: Processing your donation as you requested
• Legal Obligation: Tax reporting, ANBI/501(c)(3) compliance
• Legitimate Interests: Stewardship, impact reporting (with opt-out)
• Consent: Marketing communications, public recognition
4.3 What We Do NOT Do
GRATIS commits to:
• NEVER sell, rent, or trade donor lists or information
• NEVER share donor information with third parties for their marketing purposes
• NEVER publicly disclose donor names without explicit consent
• NEVER contact donors excessively or use aggressive fundraising tactics
5. Donor Anonymity and Recognition
5.1 Anonymous Donations
We respect your right to donate anonymously:
• You may choose to keep your identity private from the public
• Anonymous donations will not be disclosed in public reports or recognition lists
• We still record your information internally for tax receipts and compliance
• You may request we remove identifying information after a retention period
5.2 Recognition Preferences
If you consent to recognition, we may:
• List your name in annual reports or donor walls
• Recognize you at events
• Feature your story (with explicit consent)
You can change your recognition preferences at any time by contacting us.
5.3 Major Gifts
For significant donations, we may request:
• Permission to publicly acknowledge your generosity
• Consent to use your name in fundraising materials
• An interview for impact stories
All requests are optional and refusal does not affect our gratitude for your support.
6. Limited Information Sharing
We may share donor information only in these circumstances:
6.1 Service Providers
We share information with service providers who help us operate:
• Stripe/PayPal: Payment processing (PCI-DSS compliant)
• Coinbase Commerce: Cryptocurrency processing
• Email providers: Sending receipts and communications
• CRM systems: Managing donor relationships
All service providers are bound by confidentiality agreements and data processing agreements.
6.2 Legal Requirements
We may disclose information when required by:
• Valid legal process (subpoena, court order)
• Tax authorities for audit purposes
• Regulatory requirements for charitable reporting
We will notify you of such disclosures where legally permitted.
6.3 Aggregate Reporting
We may disclose aggregate, anonymized data in:
• Annual reports (total donors, total raised)
• Impact reports (funds allocated by program area)
• Grant applications
This data cannot identify individual donors.
7. Data Security
We protect donor information through:
7.1 Technical Safeguards
• Encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest (AES-256)
• PCI-DSS compliant payment processing
• Secure, access-controlled databases
• Regular security audits and vulnerability testing
• Two-factor authentication for staff accessing donor data
7.2 Administrative Safeguards
• Staff training on donor privacy and data protection
• Access granted only to staff who need it
• Confidentiality agreements with all staff and volunteers
• Written policies and procedures for data handling
7.3 Breach Response
In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting donor information:
• We will notify affected donors within 72 hours of discovery
• We will notify relevant authorities as required by law
• We will provide guidance on protective steps

9. Your Rights as a Donor
9.1 Communication Preferences
You have the right to:
• Opt out of fundraising appeals at any time
• Specify how often you wish to be contacted
• Choose communication channels (email, mail, phone)
• Request only impact updates without donation requests
9.2 Data Access and Control
You have the right to:
• Access your donor profile and giving history
• Correct inaccurate information
• Request deletion (subject to legal requirements)
• Request a copy of your data in portable format
• Withdraw consent for marketing communications
9.3 California Residents
Under CCPA/CPRA, California residents have additional rights:
• Right to know what personal information is collected and how it's used
• Right to delete personal information (subject to exceptions)
• Right to opt-out of sale/sharing (we do not sell donor information)
• Right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights
9.4 How to Exercise Rights
Contact us at privacy@gratis.ngo or donations@gratis.ngo to exercise any of these rights. We will respond within 30 days (or sooner as required by law).
10. Tax Receipts and Deductibility
10.1 Receipt Issuance
For all donations, we provide:
• Immediate email confirmation of your donation
• Annual tax summary for your records
• Specific receipts upon request
For cryptocurrency donations, receipts reflect fair market value at time of donation.
10.2 Tax-Deductibility
• Netherlands: Donations to GRATIS are tax-deductible if we maintain ANBI status
• United States: Donations may be tax-deductible when 501(c)(3) status is obtained
• Other jurisdictions: Consult your tax advisor
We cannot provide tax advice. Please consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
10.3 Required Disclosures
As required by law, tax receipts include:
• Organization's legal name and address
• KVK number (Netherlands) / EIN (US when obtained)
• Statement that no goods or services were provided (or fair market value if provided)
• Date and amount of donation
11. Children and Minor Donors
We do not knowingly solicit donations from children under 18 without parental consent. If a minor wishes to donate:
• Parental/guardian consent is required
• We recommend the donation be made by an adult on behalf of the minor
• Any communications will be sent to the adult contact
If we discover we have collected information from a minor without consent, we will delete it promptly.
12. Changes to This Policy
We may update this Donor Privacy Policy periodically. Material changes will be:
• Posted on our website
• Communicated to donors via email
• Highlighted at the top of this policy
Continued donations after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
