4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR Article 6)
We process your personal data only when we have a valid legal basis to do so. The legal bases we rely on include:
4.1 Performance of Contract (Article 6(1)(b))
We process data when necessary to perform a contract with you or take steps at your request before entering into a contract:
• Processing purchases and delivering products
• Managing your account and membership
• Providing customer support for orders
• Processing donation transactions
• Issuing tax receipts and donation confirmations
4.2 Consent (Article 6(1)(a))
We process data based on your freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent:
• Sending marketing emails and newsletters
• Placing non-essential cookies (analytics, marketing)
• Processing applications for special programs
• Publishing user-generated content with attribution
You may withdraw consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
4.3 Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f))
We process data when necessary for our legitimate interests, provided these are not overridden by your rights:
• Fraud prevention and security monitoring
• Improving our website and services
• Analyzing usage patterns to enhance user experience
• Defending legal claims
• Internal administrative purposes
We conduct balancing tests to ensure our interests do not override your fundamental rights.
4.4 Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c))
We process data when required by law:
• Tax compliance and financial reporting (Dutch tax law, IRS requirements)
• ANBI reporting requirements
• Responding to lawful requests from authorities
• Anti-money laundering obligations
• Consumer protection compliance
4.5 Public Interest (Article 6(1)(e))
As a charitable organization, certain processing may be necessary for tasks carried out in the public interest:
• Reporting on charitable impact and donations
• Transparency reporting as required for ANBI/501(c)(3) status

9. Your Rights Under GDPR
As a data subject, you have the following rights under the GDPR:
9.1 Right of Access (Article 15)
You have the right to obtain confirmation of whether we process your personal data and to receive a copy of that data along with information about how we process it.
9.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)
You have the right to have inaccurate personal data corrected and incomplete data completed.
9.3 Right to Erasure (Article 17)
You have the right to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances, including when the data is no longer necessary, you withdraw consent, or you object to processing. This right does not apply where we have a legal obligation to retain the data.
9.4 Right to Restriction (Article 18)
You have the right to request restriction of processing while we verify accuracy of data, assess an objection, or where processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction over erasure.
9.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g., JSON, CSV) and to transmit that data to another controller.
9.6 Right to Object (Article 21)
You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. For direct marketing, we will stop processing upon your objection.
9.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)
You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing that significantly affect you. We do not currently use automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects.
9.8 Right to Withdraw Consent (Article 7(3))
Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
9.9 Exercising Your Rights
To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@gratis.ngo. We will respond to your request within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request. There is no fee for exercising your rights unless requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive.
